## LAW 1: The Charlie Kirk Files Forced Disclosure Act -- Law Enforcement

**File:** `1_DoJ_FBI/Law_1_DoJ_FBI.md`

### 1. Budget Reduction Percentage Does Not Match Charter

**Charter states:** "25% automatic budget reduction per month of non-compliance"
**Law 1 actually says:** 10% per month, up to 50% cumulative (Section 7(b))

The charter specifies 25% per month. The law implements only 10% per month with a 50% cap. This is a significant weakening of the enforcement mechanism. At 25% per month, an agency would lose half its budget in 2 months. At 10% per month, it takes 5 months to reach the 50% cap. This gives non-compliant agencies substantially more time to stall.

**Fix:** Change Section 7(b) to 25% per month as specified in the charter, or update the charter to reflect the 10% figure if the reduction was intentional.

---

### 2. Schedule A Item Count Inconsistency (205 vs 208)

**Charter states:** "Now enumerates 205 specific disclosure items in Schedule A (expanded from the original 175)."
**Law 1 actually says:** "208 Critical Disclosure Items" (Schedule A heading and Section 2 purpose statement)

The charter says 205 items. The law says 208. The law appears to have been updated beyond the charter without the charter being updated. Every reference to Schedule A item counts should be consistent across the charter and all four laws.

**Fix:** Update the charter to reflect 208 items, or reconcile the law's actual item count with the charter.

---

### 3. Charter's Attorney Analysis Issue #2 (Severability) -- RESOLVED

**Charter Attorney Analysis states:** "None of the four laws contains a severability clause."
**Law 1 actual status:** Section 14 contains a severability clause.

This issue identified in the charter's own analysis has been fixed in Law 1. However, the charter's Attorney Analysis section has not been updated to reflect the fix, creating confusion about the current state of the laws.

**Fix:** Update the charter's Attorney Analysis to note this has been resolved in Law 1.

---

### 4. Charter's Attorney Analysis Issue #3 (Congressional Findings) -- RESOLVED

**Charter Attorney Analysis states:** "All four laws lack a findings section."
**Law 1 actual status:** Section 0 contains detailed Congressional Findings.

Same issue — charter analysis is outdated. Law 1 has findings covering constitutional authority, inadequacy of investigations, prior law failures, and public interest.

**Fix:** Update the charter's Attorney Analysis to note this has been resolved in Law 1.

---

### 5. Charter's Attorney Analysis Issue #4 (Special Master) -- RESOLVED

**Charter Attorney Analysis states:** "This has not been implemented."
**Law 1 actual status:** Section 8(e) includes a Special Master provision.

Law 1 has implemented the Special Master with authority to access agency systems, compel production, report to court and review board, funded by non-compliant agency.

**Fix:** Update the charter's Attorney Analysis to note this has been resolved in Law 1.

### 8. Charter's Attorney Analysis Issue #8 (Partial Passage / Standalone Fallback) -- NOT ADDRESSED

**Charter Attorney Analysis states:** Each law should include a standalone fallback provision ensuring it can operate independently even if companion statutes are not enacted.
**Law 1 actual status:** Law 1 references "any Designated Trusted Investigator under the Charlie Kirk Trusted Investigators Act" (Section 6(a)) without a fallback if Law 4 is not enacted. The definition in Section 1(g) provides a partial fallback ("or any independent investigator appointed by the review board under Section 8") but this should be more explicit.

**Fix:** Add explicit standalone operation clause: "This Act shall be fully operative regardless of whether any companion legislation is enacted."

### 9. Charter's Attorney Analysis Issue #9 (Forensic Audit Enforcement) -- NOT ADDRESSED

**Charter Attorney Analysis states:** Laws 1 and 2 require forensic audits but include no enforcement mechanism for the audit itself.
**Law 1 actual status:** Section 5(c) requires agencies to "conduct a digital forensic audit" but does not specify that refusal to conduct or complete the audit is itself a criminal offense.

**Fix:** Add provision: "Refusal to permit, conduct, or complete a forensic audit required under this section constitutes obstruction under Section 7 and is subject to the penalties therein."

### 10. Charter References "Section 12A" for Classification Prohibition but Law Uses Different Numbering

**Charter states:** "Prohibition on Classification (Section 12A)"

**Fix:** Either create a standalone Section 12A as the charter specifies, or update the charter to reflect the actual location.

## LAW 4: The Charlie Kirk Trusted Investigators Act

**File:** `4_Trusted_Investigations/Law_4_Trusted_Investigations.md`

### 1. Team Count Contradiction: Charter Says 6, Law Has 34

**Charter states:** "Six total teams (5 FBI + 5 IC)" in Team Establishment (Section 2).
**Law 4 actually establishes:** 17 Designated Trusted Investigators, each with one FBI team and one IC team = 34 total teams (17 FBI + 17 IC).

This is a major contradiction. The charter says 6 teams and lists 17 investigators. The law says 34 teams. The charter's "5 FBI + 5 IC" and "six total teams" language appears to be a remnant from an earlier draft when fewer investigators were named.

**Fix:** Update charter Section 2 to state: "34 total teams (17 FBI + 17 IC). Each of the 17 Designated Trusted Investigators receives one FBI Investigation Team and one Intelligence Investigation Team."

17 investigators × 2 teams each = 34 teams, not 6. This is an arithmetic error or outdated language in the charter.

**Fix:** Correct the charter to 34 teams.

### 3. Schedule A Item Count: Charter Says 175, Law Has 208

**Law 4 actually says:** References 208 items throughout.

**Fix:** Update charter to reference 208 items.

### 4. Charter's Attorney Analysis Issue #12 (Conflict of Interest for Trusted Investigators) -- NOT ADDRESSED

**Charter Attorney Analysis states:** "The named investigators face no financial disclosure requirements, recusal standards, or conflict-of-interest rules."
**Law 4 actual status:** No financial disclosure, recusal, or conflict-of-interest provisions have been added.

This remains unaddressed. While the charter recommends annual financial disclosures and recusal standards modeled on the Ethics in Government Act, the law has not implemented these.

**Fix:** Add provisions requiring: (a) annual public financial disclosures by each Designated Trusted Investigator, (b) recusal from investigating any matter in which the investigator has a personal financial interest, (c) prohibition on accepting gifts or payments from any person in Schedule B categories during the investigation.

### 6. Charter's Attorney Analysis Issue #8 (Standalone Fallback) -- NOT ADDRESSED

**Charter states:** Each law should include standalone fallback provisions.

### 7. Budget Amount May Be Insufficient for 17 Investigators

**Charter states:** "Independent budget for legal counsel, forensic experts, and security" (no amount specified).
**Law 4 actually specifies:** $5M per Designated Trusted Investigator ($85M total) plus "additional sums as necessary."

While the law specifies amounts, the charter does not. More importantly, $5M per investigator covering two full investigation teams (FBI + IC), independent legal counsel, forensic experts, AND a personal security detail may be insufficient. Law 3 allocates $10M per team for agency-run investigations. Law 4 allocates $5M for two teams plus additional overhead.

**Fix:** Either increase per-investigator allocation to $10M (matching Law 3's per-team allocation) or ensure the "additional sums" language is adequate to cover security details and expert costs separately.

### 10. No Mechanism for Trusted Investigators to Compel Testimony Under Oath

**Fix:** Add provision: "Trusted Investigators may conduct interviews under oath. False statements made under oath to a Designated Trusted Investigator or investigation team shall be prosecutable under 18 U.S.C. 1001 (false statements) and 18 U.S.C. 1621 (perjury)."
