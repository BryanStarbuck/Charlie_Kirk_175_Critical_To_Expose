## Cross-Cutting Issues — REMAINING UNFIXED

### 6. Constitutional Vulnerabilities (Presidential Override) — PARTIALLY FIXED

**What's fixed:** Law 3 Section 7B has implemented proper constitutional guardrails: presidential assertions reviewed by court within 14 days, presumption against privilege, burden on asserting party.

**What remains unfixed:** Laws 1 and 2 still use an absolute prohibition approach to presidential override without the procedural review mechanism implemented in Law 3. This creates a separation-of-powers vulnerability specifically for Laws 1 and 2. Law 2 is especially vulnerable because the President's Article II authority over national security is strongest regarding the intelligence community.

**What needs to happen:** Laws 1 and 2 should adopt the same procedural approach used in Law 3 Section 7B: require presidential assertions be reviewed by a court within 14 days, with presumption against privilege and burden of proof on the asserting party (clear and convincing evidence standard).

---

### 7. Repeated Disclaimers — NOT FIXED

**What remains unfixed:** All four laws still contain disclaimer paragraphs embedded within operative statutory text. These disclaimers state "This document does not assert as fact that any individual... has committed any criminal, illegal, or immoral act." In actual legislation, a single disclaimer in the findings or preamble section suffices. Repeated disclaimers in operative text are unusual and may undermine legislative credibility — an opposing DOJ could argue "Congress itself disclaimed that any wrongdoing occurred — so why is this disclosure necessary?"

**What needs to happen:** Consolidate all disclaimers to a single location in the Section 0 (Congressional Findings) or preamble section of each law. Remove all other instances from operative text.

---

### 8. Partial Passage Risk (Standalone Fallback) — PARTIALLY FIXED

**What's fixed:** Laws 1, 2, and 4 now contain standalone operation clauses:
- Law 1 Section 11(b): "This Act shall apply independently..."
- Law 2 Section 12F(b): "This Act shall be fully operative and enforceable regardless..."
- Law 4 Section 15D(c): Comprehensive fallback with authority vesting in Designated Trusted Investigators.

**What remains unfixed:** Law 3 appears to lack an explicit standalone operation clause. If Laws 1 and 2 are not enacted, Law 3's investigation mandates reference disclosure mechanisms from those companion statutes without a clear fallback.

**What needs to happen:** Add an explicit standalone operation clause to Law 3 ensuring it can function independently if the companion disclosure statutes are not enacted.
